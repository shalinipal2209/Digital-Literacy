PART B: “STAY – SAFE ONLINE” CHECKLIST

1. Use strong, unique passwords 
Create different passwords for each account and include a mix of letters, 
numbers, and symbols. Avoid using names or birthdates. 

2. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) 
Turn on 2FA for email, social media, and banking apps to add an extra layer 
of security. 

3. Never share OTPs or PINs 
Banks and apps will never ask for your OTP, UPI PIN, or passwords—don’t 
share them with anyone.
 
4. Be cautious with unknown links and QR codes 
Don’t click suspicious links or scan random QR codes, especially those 
claiming rewards, refunds, or urgent actions.
 
5.  Verify before accepting UPI requests 
Always check the sender’s name and purpose before approving any 
payment request on apps like Google Pay or PhonePe.
 
6. Remember: receiving money doesn’t require a PIN 
If someone asks you to enter your UPI PIN to “receive” money, it’s a scam.
 
7. Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions 
Don’t log into banking or make payments on unsecured networks in cafes 
or campuses. 

8. Limit personal information on social media 
Oversharing details like phone numbers, college ID, or location can make 
you an easy target. 

9. Install apps only from official stores 
Download apps from trusted sources like Google Play Store and avoid 
unofficial APK files. 

REPORT CYBER CRIME IMMEDIATELY: 
If you fall victim to any online fraud:   
Report at: National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in)  
Call Helpline: 1930 (for immediate financial fraud assistance)
